How It Works
Three steps to anti-inflation. Each round is identical — 10 SOL in, tokens out.
01
Pay 10 SOL
Every mint round costs exactly 10 SOL. Payment goes to the project treasury. Same price, every round, forever.
02
50% to You, 50% to Vault
Half the tokens go to your wallet. The other half enters the stability vault. Earlier rounds yield exponentially more tokens.
03
Vault Sells at $1
When market price exceeds $1, the vault sells at exactly $1.00 — a hard price ceiling. The floor rises with each round.

    Answers
    FAQ
    Common questions about TOBE, answered directly.
    Is $TOBE a stablecoin? +
    No. $TOBE is an anti-inflationary token designed to approach $1 — not peg to it. The vault mechanism creates upward pressure toward $1, but the market determines the actual price. Think of $1 as a ceiling the protocol builds toward, not a guarantee.
    What happens after all 1,024 rounds are minted? +
    Minting stops permanently. No more tokens can ever be created. The vault continues to hold 50% of all tokens ever minted, selling only at the SOL ceiling. The token becomes fully deflationary — fixed supply, growing demand floor.
    Can the team rug or change the rules? +
    No. The smart contract controls all minting and vault operations through PDAs (Program Derived Addresses). The authority can pause/unpause for emergencies and update the treasury address, but cannot mint extra tokens, drain the vault, or change the supply curve. All actions are verifiable on-chain.
    Why does each round cost exactly 10 SOL? +
    Every round costs the same — 10 SOL. What changes is how many tokens you receive. Round 1 gives you 524,288 tokens. Round 1024 gives you 512. Same price, fewer tokens. This is what makes early participation more rewarding and creates the anti-inflationary curve.
    Where does the SOL go when I mint? +
    50% (5 SOL) goes to the protocol treasury to fund development, liquidity, and operations. The other 50% (5 SOL) funds the vault token allocation that creates the price floor mechanism. Every transaction is trackable on-chain.
    Can I sell my $TOBE tokens? +
    Yes. $TOBE is a standard SPL token — you can transfer, trade, or sell it freely on any Solana DEX once liquidity is established. There are no transfer restrictions or sell taxes.
    Is there a team allocation or pre-mine? +
    Zero. There are no team tokens, no advisor tokens, no VC rounds, no pre-mine. Every single $TOBE token enters circulation through the same 1,024 public mint rounds. The founder mints under the same rules as everyone else.
    What is the vault and how does it work? +
    Every time someone mints, 50% of the tokens go to the minter and 50% go to a PDA-controlled vault. The vault only releases tokens when the market price hits the SOL ceiling — creating permanent buy pressure. The vault grows with every mint and acts as a floor mechanism for the token.
    How is the token price determined? +
    The market determines the price — $TOBE trades freely on DEXs like any other token. What the protocol controls is the cost per token at mint, which increases every round as fewer tokens are issued. The vault creates upward pressure toward $1, but the actual trading price is set by supply and demand.
    What network is TOBE on? +
    Solana. We chose Solana for sub-second finality, near-zero transaction fees, and a mature SPL token ecosystem. Minting costs a fraction of a cent in gas — compared to $10-$50+ on Ethereum.
    Do I need anything besides 10 SOL to mint? +
    Yes — you'll need a small amount of SOL (less than 0.01 SOL) to cover Solana transaction fees, plus a Solana wallet like Phantom, Backpack, or Solflare. That's it. No KYC, no sign-up, no approval process.
    Why should I trust this over other tokens? +
    Don't trust — verify. The smart contract is open source. Every mint, vault deposit, and token transfer is on-chain and displayed on this page in real time. There's no team allocation, no backdoor, no admin mint function. The rules are set by code, not by people. Math, not marketing.
    What's stopping the price from going below my cost? +
    Nothing is guaranteed — this is crypto, not a bank account. What the protocol does is create structural upward pressure: each round mints fewer tokens, the vault accumulates supply that only sells at $1, and no new tokens can be created outside the 1,024 rounds. These mechanics push toward stability, but the market has the final say.

    Liquidity
    Raydium Integration
    How $TOBE goes from minting to a live trading pair — trustlessly, with locked liquidity.
    1

    Minting Fills the Pool Reserve

    Every mint costs 10 SOL — half the tokens go to the minter, half go to the vault. The accumulated SOL treasury seeds the Raydium liquidity pool.

    2

    Vault Tokens Pair with SOL

    The vault holds 50% of all minted $TOBE. When pool seeding triggers, these vault tokens are paired with SOL from the treasury to create the $TOBE / SOL liquidity pool on Raydium.

    3

    Pool Seeded On-Chain

    The seed_pool instruction is called once by the authority. It transfers vault tokens + SOL from treasury into a Raydium concentrated liquidity pool. This is a one-time, irreversible action — verified on-chain.

    4

    LP Tokens Locked for 2 Years

    The LP tokens received from Raydium are immediately locked via the lock_lp instruction. No rug pulls — liquidity cannot be removed until the lock expires. The unlock timestamp is stored on-chain and publicly verifiable.

    5

    Live Trading Begins

    Once the pool is live, anyone can trade $TOBE on Raydium. The vault's remaining tokens create a price floor — they only release at $1.00, providing structural buy pressure and stability.

    Why TOBE Exists

    Crypto promised to give everyone a fair shot. Instead, it created a new class of insiders — teams that pre-mint millions of tokens, VCs that get discounted allocations, and influencers that pump and dump on their own followers. The average person enters late, buys high, and holds the bag. That's not innovation. That's the same old game with a new coat of paint.

    TOBE was built on a different principle: value should be earned, not extracted. There are no team tokens. No private rounds. No seed investors with 10x advantages. Every single token enters circulation the same way — through 1,024 public mint rounds that anyone can participate in, at a price set by math, not marketing.

    The mechanism is simple. Each round, you pay 10 SOL. Half the tokens go to you. The other half goes to a vault that only sells at the SOL ceiling — creating permanent buy pressure beneath the price. Every round mints fewer tokens than the last, so early participants get more, but late participants pay the same. The supply curve is decreasing, not inflating. The vault grows, not shrinks.

    There is no admin key that can change the rules. No multisig that can drain the vault. No governance vote that can inflate the supply. The smart contract is the protocol, and the protocol is final. Every mint, every vault deposit, every release — all verifiable on Solana, all displayed on this page in real time.

    TOBE isn't trying to be the next moonshot. It's trying to be something crypto has very few of: a token that does exactly what it says it will do.
